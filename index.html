Abstract

We present a new approach for modeling and animating dynamics from a single still picture. Our focus is real-world scenes with natural oscillation motions such as trees, flowers, clothes, or candle flames moving in the breeze. Most state-of-the-art video animation methods typically predicts video frames directly, producing animation of short time duration, low frame rate, exhibiting unrealistic and temporal inconsistent motions or textures. Based on the observation that natural oscillation motions can be modeled efficiently and accurately in frequency space, we present Neural Stochastic Motion Textures, a new motion representation that models per-pixel motion trajectory of input picture within Fourier domain. We learn the motion priors through frequency latent co-denoising model from a large collections of videos, so that during inference, predicted stochastic motion textures are used to animate future frames via image-based rendering. We show that our approach produces photo-realistic animation of much higher generation quality compared with prior baselines, and demonstrate that neural stochastic motion textures enables a variety of motion and video editing effects.
